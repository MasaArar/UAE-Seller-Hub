How Amazon Fees & Profitability Work

Understanding your cost structure is the foundation of a profitable Amazon business.

📊

Amazon Referral Fee

Amazon charges a referral fee on every sale, typically 8%–15% depending on the product category. Electronics are usually lower; beauty and accessories are higher. Always check your specific category.

🧾

UAE VAT (5%)

The UAE applies a 5% VAT on most products. If you're a VAT-registered seller, this is collected from the buyer — but it still affects your pricing strategy and net receipts.

📦

FBA vs FBM Shipping

If you use Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA), your shipping cost includes FBA fees. If self-fulfilled (FBM), include your actual courier cost. This significantly impacts your margin calculation.

💰

Why Margin Matters

A healthy Amazon margin is typically 20–30%+. Below 15%, it becomes hard to run ads (PPC) profitably. Always calculate margin before sourcing any product.

📈

ROI for Sellers

ROI measures how much return you get on your invested capital (cost of goods + shipping). A 50%+ ROI per product cycle is considered strong for Amazon FBA sellers.

⚠️

Hidden Costs to Watch

Don't forget: PPC advertising, return rates, storage fees, and product photography. Add these to your cost structure for a realistic profit picture before you commit to a product.
